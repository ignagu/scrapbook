How I built this website

In an era where multiple services (wix, wordpress, ...) exist to make a websites "effortlessly" I chose to create this website based on a site template that I downloaded on html5up.net Why? I wouldn't have learned anything on wix, wordpress,... and in that case I might just post on Instagram & Facebook. I also made this decisions for technical reasons I won't explain...

I hosted this website on github.com because it's free. That's another advantage of not using the websites I mentioned before. No need to pay. If you want to know how to do it watch this Youtube video.

Who needs a proper domain name? When you're starting a website/blog you don't need one. This will save you some money (again). Just customize your "github domain".

Don't forget to optimize your images, use tinypng.com.

I want to thank Andres Castillo for motivating me to build this website and giving me all the tips and tricks I mentioned before! - Go check out his website.

Photography Gear

Here's a quick list of photography gear! I'm still working on it. Every product is linked below!

Camera gear

Nikon - D7500 + NIKKOR 18-140mm f/3.5-5.6

I had a Nikon D5000 for 10 years and it was time to update. This Nikon D7500 is my new buddy... I'll share pictures taken with this camera very soon.

Canon - Powershot G5 X

If you need a camera for light travels, this one is your camera. You'll thank me later for that viewfinder in super bright conditions (beach, hiking,...).

Lenses

Tamron - 70-300mm f/4-5.6

Sometimes you just can't get close enough to what you would like to shoot. If you're an amateur photographer, go for this lens for your far away shots. The sealion pictures at La Baie de Somme were shot with this lens.

Yongnuo - 50mm f/1.8

If you want to shot portraits and not spend a huge amount of money check out this 50mm lens. Lots of lights will pop into your lens producing that blurry effect you're looking for. The build of the lens feels cheap but if you're careful with your gear it's fine. By the way, sometimes you will get funny effects on your pictures but it adds an unexpected "artsy" touch. Have fun.

Joby GorillaPod 3K

I don't like tripods. But I found an alternative. This product is your man if you hate caring around bulky stuff. I also use it to hold my iPad upright in bed while I "Netxflix & Chill".

SD Card Reader

TedGem SD Card Reader is perfect on the go! Make sure to store pictures on your phone/computer while you're traveling. This affoardable solution can save you precious memories. Be careful, maje sure you buy a card reader sutaible for your smartphone (micro USB, USB-C, ...).
